Child health & nutrition – WASH-focused prototype

This prototype uses Global Burden of Disease (GBD 2023) data to show how different causes affect children at different ages, and to estimate how much of that burden is linked to water, sanitation and hygiene (WASH).

It also pulls national stunting and wasting estimates from the World Bank API (UNICEF/WHO/WB JME series) to give a separate nutrition view, and visually juxtaposes WASH-related under-five burden with under-five stunting/wasting.

  • Health (GBD) view: child deaths/DALYs by age and cause, grouped into WASH Core, WASH Extended and Non-WASH.
  • Nutrition view: stunting and wasting trends for under-fives (JME series), with a simple under-five WASH–nutrition snapshot.
  • WASH Core: causes strongly driven by faecal–oral transmission or unsafe excreta management (diarrhoea, intestinal nematodes, typhoid, etc.).
  • WASH Extended: conditions where WASH, hygiene and IPC are important but not the only drivers (respiratory infections, neonatal/maternal sepsis, some skin/infection conditions).
  • Non-WASH: all other causes, shown to keep the denominator correct and highlight where WASH is not the main lever.

This is an internal UNICEF analytical tool to support WASH, health and nutrition dialogue. Values are indicative and should be interpreted together with national surveillance and programme data.
